## Summary

The Marks Family Trust property's homeowners insurance (Liberty Mutual) lapsed in mid-2024, creating a months-long gap in coverage. This triggered EastRise Credit Union to place Collateral Protection Insurance (CPI) on the property — a more expensive, less comprehensive policy forced by the lender to protect its mortgage collateral. The insurance situation involved multiple attorneys, an insurance broker, and was still not fully resolved as of December 2025.

## Evidentiary Significance

1. **Trustee duty to insure trust property** — letting coverage lapse exposes trust assets to catastrophic loss
2. **Cost to trust** — CPI is typically 2-5x more expensive than standard homeowners insurance, with less coverage
3. **Extended timeline** — from first lapse (Jun 2024) to Dec 2025 is 18 months of insurance chaos
4. **Documentation trail** — EastRise's formal demand, Liberty Mutual's notices, and multiple attorney threads all preserved
5. **Settlor intent** — Peterson's July 2025 email explicitly addresses "musings on Settlor intent" regarding insurance
